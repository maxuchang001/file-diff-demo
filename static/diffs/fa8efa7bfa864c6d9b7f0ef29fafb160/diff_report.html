Summary of Differences

The image illustrates two distinct interface connections:

  • Left Connection: AXI4 master APB slave
  • Right Connection: AXI4 master AHB slave

The key difference lies in the interface used by the slave:

  • APB (Advanced Peripheral Bus)
  • AHB (Advanced High-Performance Bus)

Both connections originate from an AXI4 master, but the target slave differs:

  • APB: Typically used for lower-speed peripherals.
  • AHB: Used for high-speed peripherals and interconnects.

In summary, the difference is between using APB and AHB as the slave interface, with APB being slower and AHB supporting higher performance.
